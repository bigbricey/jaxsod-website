## The Science of Rooting: What's Happening Underground?

To understand the timeline, you need to understand the plant's biology. When sod is harvested at the farm, up to **80% of its root system is sliced off**. The grass arrives at your home in a state of shock.

For the first few weeks, the grass is running on stored energy (carbohydrates) in its leaves and stolons. It is frantically trying to grow new "adventitious roots" to anchor itself and begin drawing water from your soil before its energy reserves run out.

**This is a race against time.** If the roots don't establish before the stored energy is depleted, the sod dies.

## The 60-Day Establishment Timeline

### Days 1-7: The "Life Support" Phase (Critical)

## Factors That Speed Up (or Slow Down) Rooting

The timeline above isn't a guaranteed law of physics; it's a rule of thumb. Nature has variables.

### 1. Soil Temperature (The #1 Factor)
Grass roots grow based on soil temperature, not air temperature.
- **Optimal (75¬∞F - 85¬∞F):** *Late Spring / Early Summer.* Roots grow aggressively. Establishment can happen in as little as 14 days.
- **Slow (60¬∞F - 70¬∞F):** *Fall / Early Spring.* Rooting takes 30-45 days.
- **Stalled (<55¬∞F):** *Winter (Dormancy).* If you install sod in January in Jacksonville, it may not root *at all* until March. It will just sit there. This is fine, as long as you keep it moist, but do not walk on it for months.

### 2. Variety Matters
Not all grass species are equal:

### 3. Soil Prep
Did you rototill? Did you add topsoil?
- **Compacted Clay/Hardpan:** Roots struggle to penetrate. Takes 2x longer.
- **Loamy/Sandy Soil:** Roots dive right in. Much faster establishment.

## Troubleshooting: "Why isn't my sod rooting?"

It‚Äôs been 3 weeks, and the sod still lifts up like a cheap rug. What‚Äôs wrong?

### 1. The "Air Gap" of Death
If the ground wasn‚Äôt leveled or the sod wasn‚Äôt rolled after installation, there may be air pockets between the sod and the soil. Roots cannot grow through air.
*   **Fix:** heavy watering and walking on the specific loose spots to press them down.

### 2. The Drying Cycle
If the sod dried out heavily on Day 3 or 4, the initial root nodes died. The plant has to "re-boot" and try again, setting you back a week.
*   **Fix:** Increase watering consistency.

### 3. The Shadow Effect
Sod planted in deep shade (under big Oaks) directs energy to leaf survival (reaching for light) rather than root growth. Shade sod always takes longer to root.

## FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I fertilize to make it root faster?**
**A:** **NO.** Do not use high-nitrogen fertilizer on new sod for at least 30 days. You will force the grass to grow tall (blades) instead of deep (roots), and you risk chemical burn on the tender young tissues. Use a "Starter Fertilizer" (high Phosphorus) only if your soil test recommends it, or just stick to water.

**Q: My dog needs to go out. What do I do?**
**A:** This is the hardest part. You must leash-walk your dog to a designated spot (preferably mulch or a hidden corner) for the first 14 days. Large dogs running on wet, unrooted sod will tear the seams apart, and their urine causes instant dead spots on new grass.

**Q: It rained today. Do I still need to run the sprinklers?**
**A:** Maybe. A light drizzle does nothing. You need at least 0.5 inches of water to soak the sod. Buy a cheap rain gauge. If you got a heavy Florida thunderstorm, turn the system off for the day. If it just sprinkled, run the cycle.

**Q: When can I treat for weeds?**
**A:** Wait at least 60-90 days. Pre-emergents and weed killers are stressful to the plant. If you spray them on a rootless lawn, you can stunt it permanently. Hand-pull weeds for the first 3 months.
