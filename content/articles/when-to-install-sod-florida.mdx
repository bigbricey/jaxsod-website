## 2. Summer (June â€“ August)
**Status:** The High-Risk / High-Reward Zone.
**Rating:** â­â­â­ (Good, but stressful)

Grass loves heat + water. It grows incredibly fast in summer. But sod on a pallet is a ticking time bomb.
- **Pros:** Fastest establishment. You can have a rooted lawn in 10 days.
- **Cons:** **The "Cook" Factor.** If sod sits on the pallet for 24 hours, it will compost and die. You absolutely MUST have your irrigation dialed in. If you miss one day of watering, the lawn dies.
- **Risks:** Fungus pressure is high due to humidity.
- **Strategy:** Install immediately upon delivery. Double-check your sprinkler coverage before the grass arrives.

**Rating:** â­â­â­â­â­ (Excellent)

Locals know this is the best time to garden. The air is drier, pests are dying off, but the ground is still warm.
- **Pros:** Less water required than summer. Less weed competition.
- **Cons:** Shorter days mean slower leaf growth.
- **Strategy:** Great for St. Augustine replacement. Get it down before Thanksgiving so it roots before the first frost.

## 4. Winter (December â€“ February)
**Status:** The "Dormant" Install.
**Rating:** â­â­â­ (Safe, but ugly)

Can you lay sod in January? Yes. New construction homes do it every day.
- **Pros:** **Lowest maintenance.** You almost never have to water it. It just sits there. It won't die from lack of water.
- **Cons:** **It won't turn green.** It will lay there, brown/dormant, until March. Roots grow very slowly.
- **Risk:** If we get a hard freeze (20s) *right* after install, the tender roots can be damaged.
- **Strategy:** If you have aMud pit, do it. It stops erosion. If you want instant beauty, wait for March.

## Summary: When should YOU install?

**If you have an Irrigation System:**
You can install **any time**. Just adjust your water schedule (Heavy in Summer, Light in Winter).

**If you depend on Hose Sprinklers:**
Wait for the **Rainy Season (June-August)** or **Spring**. Trying to hand-water a lawn in a dry October is misery.

**If you are selling your house:**
Do it **NOW**. A green lawn adds immediate value, regardless of the season.
