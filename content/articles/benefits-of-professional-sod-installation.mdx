## 2. Site Prep: The Secret to Longevity

You can put fresh carpet on a broken floor, but it will still be uneven. The same applies to sod.
- **DIY Prep:** Usually involves spraying RoundUp (which takes 2 weeks to work) and maybe raking the surface.
- **Pro Prep:** We use **Sod Cutters** to physically slice off the old layer of weeds and grass. We don't just cover the weeds; we remove them.
- **Grading:** We smooth the soil surface to remove ankle-breaking holes and lumps, ensuring a "golf course" finish that is easy to mow later.

## 3. The "Freshness" Factor

Sod is perishable produce. It has a shelf life of about 24-48 hours once cut.
- **Big Box Stores:** That pallet at the garden center might have been cut 3 days ago. It's already yellowing in the center.
- **Professional Supply Chain:** We coordinate directly with the local farms. Your sod is often cut at 4:00 AM and installed in your yard by 11:00 AM on the **same day**. This freshness guarantees a faster, healthier root-in.

## 4. Technical Expertise (The Art of the Seam)

There is a technique to laying sod that prevents gaps and washouts.
- **Brick Pattern:** We stagger the seams like brickwork so water can't create channels and wash the soil away.
- **Tight Seams:** We hand-tuck edges ensuring no air gaps (which kill roots) and no overlaps (which cause scalping).
- **Rolling:** We use heavy water-rollers after installation to press the sod into the soil, eliminating air pockets and ensuring capillary action for water uptake.
